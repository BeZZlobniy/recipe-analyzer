Personalized recipe analysis should start from the user's raw profile text, not only from normalized labels. Treat diet type, goal, allergies, diseases, preferences, and free-text restrictions as separate constraints. For each constraint, compare the recipe ingredients, hidden ingredient sources, cooking method, sodium, calories, protein, fat, carbohydrates, and recommended portion size.

Allergies and strict exclusions require ingredient-level checks. Direct sources and hidden sources both matter: dairy can appear as milk, cream, cheese, sour cream, butter, yogurt, whey, casein, cottage cheese, and processed cheese; gluten can appear as wheat flour, pasta, bread, breadcrumbs, semolina, wheat groats, sauces, and breading; egg can appear in dough, batters, mayonnaise, and baked goods; fish and shellfish should be separated because a person may react to one group but not the other.

Medical conditions should be interpreted as risk factors for the recipe, not as a diagnosis. Hypertension and kidney-related salt restrictions usually require attention to sodium, salty seasoning, pickles, marinades, cheese, processed meat, and sauces. Diabetes or insulin resistance requires attention to added sugar, honey, sweet sauces, refined flour, pasta, potatoes, rice, and the total carbohydrate load per portion. Hyperlipidemia and cardiovascular risk require attention to saturated fat, fatty pork, butter, cream, sour cream, cheese, fried foods, and total fat. Gout or high uric acid often requires caution with large amounts of red meat, organ meats, some fish, rich broths, and alcohol.

Подагра и высокий уровень мочевой кислоты: основной пищевой риск связан не с натрием, а с пуриновой нагрузкой. В рецептах стоит отдельно отмечать свинину, говядину, красное мясо, субпродукты, крепкие мясные бульоны, некоторые виды рыбы и большие мясные порции. Если одновременно указано "не ем свинину", свинина является прямым конфликтом предпочтения/ограничения и дополнительным поводом предложить замену.

Free-text preferences and restrictions should be matched semantically. If a user writes that they avoid spicy food, look for chili, hot pepper, adjika, wasabi, tabasco, hot sauces, and large amounts of black or red pepper. If a user writes "no pork", pork, bacon, ham, lard, and mixed minced meat can conflict. If a user writes "less fried food", deep-frying or pan-frying in much oil should be highlighted even when the ingredients themselves are acceptable.

When evidence is uncertain, explain uncertainty and give a practical check instead of claiming a hard conflict. For example, "seasoning for chicken may contain salt or milk powder; check the label" is better than a generic warning. Recommendations should be concrete: replace the ingredient, reduce the quantity, change the cooking method, or split the full recipe into more portions.
